    • Why GIS?

    As society becomes increasingly reliant on spatial data, the demand for professionals skilled in Geographic Information Systems (GIS) is growing rapidly. This is driven by factors like increasing urbanization, environmental concerns, and the need for accurate spatial data in various industries. Thus, GIS professionals are needed for tasks like mapping, data analysis, resource management, and urban planning. According to the US Bureau of Labor Statistics, the demand for GIS professionals is projected to grow by 13% between 2020 and 2030. This makes GIS indispensable for many industries as GIS software supports the mapping of field operations, integration of analytical tools, the management & display of geographic information through dashboards and reports.

    Why Drone Technology?

    Drones offer cost-effective and efficient solutions for tasks like aerial mapping, surveying, crop monitoring, and package delivery, which enhances both productivity and safety. Thus, the demand for its operations and use creates a need for skilled drone pilots, data analysts, and software developers in this field. The drone market projected to grow to $43.4 billion by 2024, with applications in sectors like agriculture, construction, inspection, and delivery.

    Why 3D Design/Printing?

    3D printing, also known as additive manufacturing, is revolutionizing manufacturing by allowing for the creation of complex and customized products on-demand. Factors accelerating the growth of the market include the ease in development of customized products and reduction in manufacturing cost and process downtime. These factors have the potential to disrupt entire industries and create new possibilities for product design and development. The global 3D printing market is projected to reach $35.4 billion by 2026, with applications in healthcare, aerospace, automotive, and consumer goods. This growth necessitates the demand for skilled personnel in design, engineering, and operation of 3D printers.
